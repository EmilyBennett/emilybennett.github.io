Hack the Mask

Mask Adapter for 3D printing and laser cutting

About

This design improves on the open source design at fixthemask.com with a 3d-printable nose strap, which improves the fit of the rubber band to the face and reduces pressure on the nose. The design uses a custom clip which ensures the strap can be inserted and not become loose during the course of the day. It also includes a modified rubber mask template which allows for additional length to fit over the nose piece. This design could be mass-manufacturered on-side by using local 3D printers.

Instructions

Materials:
-1 x Rubber Sheet
-3D printer and filament

Step 1 - Component #1 - Nose Adapter

Print the nose clip using a 3D printer.

Step 2 - Component #2 - Rubber Mask Adapter

Print at 100% and draw around template onto a rubber sheet. Cut out with a pair of scissors. Modified design from fixthemask.com.

Step 3 - Assemble Components

Hook the strap through the nose clip at each side.
