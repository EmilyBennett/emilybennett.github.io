Speedy Bio

incubation in the post for speedy results

Laboratory space is expensive

In a post-COVID world, research labs are stretched to capacity. Social distancing means that fewer researchers can work at the bench at the same time.

A researcher's time is valuable

Researchers don't want to spend time on repetitive tasks which are easily automatable

Quicker results = faster innovation

Increasing the turnaround speed for building to sequencing allows scientists to make decisions faster

We are building a low-cost incubator that you can send in the post







A low cost solution

Researchers working in molecular biology laboratories often need to read the sequence of DNA in bacterial cells. This involves a labour intensive step of growing those cells for 2 days, first overnight on solid media, then in liquid media in order to amplify the DNA so it can be read by a sequencing machine. This takes up a lot of researcher time and laboratory space. We propose that by sending samples in a low-cost insulated envelope with an inbuilt temperature controller we can save time and resources and offer a way for researchers to receive their results faster.

Emily Bennett James Lawson 2020
